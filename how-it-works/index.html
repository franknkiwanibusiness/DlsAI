How escrow works
Money never moves until both sides confirm
Funds go into a locked escrow vault the moment an offer is accepted — neither party can access them until the transfer is confirmed complete.

Offer accepted → funds lock
The moment a seller accepts an offer, the exact offer amount is moved from the buyer's wallet into Siterifty escrow. Neither party can access it. The clock starts on asset transfer.
Seller transfers all assets
Domain, codebase, databases, credentials — the seller transfers everything agreed in the listing. This typically takes 24–72 hours. Communication happens on-platform for a full record.
Buyer confirms delivery
The buyer verifies everything arrived correctly — DNS, code access, database credentials, associated accounts. Only when the buyer taps Confirm does escrow release. If something's missing, raise a dispute first.
Payout released instantly
Escrow releases to the seller's Siterifty wallet immediately after buyer confirmation. The seller can withdraw, reinvest in new listings, or put it toward their next plan payment — all in one place.
Dispute protection
Something went wrong?
If the seller fails to deliver assets — or the assets don't match the listing — open a dispute from your dashboard before confirming delivery. Our team reviews all on-platform evidence and can release escrow back to the buyer. Keep all communication on-platform so there's a documented record.
