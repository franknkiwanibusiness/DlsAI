Questions
What can my wallet balance be used for?
Your balance can be used for site purchases, monthly plan upgrades (Starter, Growth, Pro), managed hosting fees, and any future credits or promotions we offer. It never expires.
Can I withdraw my balance?
Wallet balances are non-refundable and cannot be withdrawn to a bank or PayPal account. The balance is store credit only. If you believe there's been an error, contact support.
Why is there a processing fee?
PayPal charges a standard processing fee of 3.49% + $0.49 for each transaction. This fee goes directly to PayPal — Siterifty does not keep any of it.
Is this payment secure?
Yes. All payments are processed by PayPal using 256-bit SSL encryption. We never store your card details. Your payment is covered by PayPal Buyer Protection.
Does my balance expire?
No. Your wallet balance never expires. You can top up whenever you like and spend at your own pace with no time pressure.

Common Questions
How do I list a site for sale?
Go to the main marketplace and tap + List a Site. Fill in the details — URL, revenue, traffic, asking price — and submit. Your listing goes live immediately on the Free plan (1 slot) or up to your plan limit.
What payment methods are accepted?
We currently process payments through PayPal. All site purchases and subscription payments are handled securely through PayPal's checkout. Your wallet balance can be used toward any purchase.
What are the platform fees?
Free plan: 30% fee per sale. Starter: 15%. Growth: 10%. Pro: 5% — the lowest on the platform. Upgrade anytime from the Account tab to keep more of what you earn.
Can I cancel my subscription?
Yes, anytime. Go to Settings → Billing and switch to the Free plan. Your paid features remain active until the end of your current billing period. No penalties, no hassle.
How does wallet balance work?
Top up your wallet with any amount via PayPal. Your balance is then used for site purchases, monthly plan payments, and hosting fees — all in one place. Balances never expire and are non-refundable store credit.
